# King Rhoam / Old Man -- Complete Dialogue Reference

*Source: The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (2017)*
*Compiled from: Zelda Wiki, Zelda Dungeon Wiki, GameFAQs, IMDb, fan transcriptions*
*Focus: Gameplay text boxes (not voice-acted cutscenes)*

---

## Design Context for Merlin

King Rhoam's "Old Man" persona is the design template for Merlin's personality. Key traits:
- **Guides without commanding** -- nudges, hints, rhetorical questions
- **Playful mischief** -- teases, jokes, feigns offense, then laughs
- **Warmth disguised as gruffness** -- campfire wisdom, not lectures
- **Earns trust through presence** -- he's just *there*, available when you come to him
- **Never reveals his full hand** -- information is earned, not dumped

## 1. First Encounter -- Campfire Near the Shrine of Resurrection

### The Greeting

> "Oho ho! Well met, stranger! It's rather unusual to see another soul in these parts."

### Who Are You?

> "Me? I'll spare you my life story. I'm just an old fool who has lived here, alone, for quite some time now. What brings a bright-eyed young man like you to a place like this?"

### Where Am I?

> "Answering a question with a question. That is fair enough. As I cannot imagine our meeting to be a simple coincidence... I shall tell you."

> "This is the Great Plateau. According to legend, this is the birthplace of the entire kingdom of Hyrule."

### About the Temple

> "That temple there... Long ago, it was the site of many sacred ceremonies. Ever since the decline of the kingdom 100 years ago, it has sat abandoned, in a state of decay."

### The Campfire Mood

> "Oh... I was just feeling a chill. A warm fire can do wonders for a weary soul."

> "A campfire is a wonderful place to pass the time. Staring into these flames, it's easy to lose track of the hours..."

> "Bah! Please ignore my ramblings. Feel free to relax here for as long as you'd like."

### Nonverbal Cues
- Opens with "Oho ho!" -- his signature exclamation, delighted surprise
- Speaks seated by a fire, never standing over Link
- Offers the space ("Feel free to relax here")

## 2. The Baked Apple Incident -- Teaching Through Play

When Link takes the Old Man's Baked Apple from the campfire:

> "I BEG YOUR PARDON! I do believe that is my baked apple! You can't just go about taking whatever you please."

*[Beat]*

> "Oho ho! Forgive me -- I could not resist pulling your leg. Please help yourself."

### The Cooking Lesson

> "An Apple and an open flame make for a succulent treat."

> "Just a few tricks I've picked up after many, many years in the wild... You may take my advice or leave it."

### Nonverbal Cues
- Feigns outrage, then immediately laughs
- "Oho ho!" -- the tell that he's playing, not angry
- "Take my advice or leave it" -- never commands

---

## 3. Tree Chopping -- Teaching By Doing

The Old Man is found near his cabin, attempting to chop down a tree. If Link inquires:

> "I want to use this tree for firewood."

When Link helps:

> "The trick is to turn your hips so that they face where you want the tree to land."

### Nonverbal Cues
- He doesn't ask for help directly -- he's just *doing the thing*
- Teaching is embedded in the activity, not a separate lecture
- Practical wisdom: "turn your hips" -- body knowledge, not theory

---

## 4. The Torch and Fire

> "If you need to start a fire, just light your torch on that campfire over there."

### Hidden Dialogue -- Setting Him on Fire

If Link sets the Old Man on fire with a wood bundle:

> "Toasty."

### Nonverbal Cues
- Completely deadpan reaction to being set on fire
- One word. No scolding. Pure character.

## 5. After the Great Plateau Tower -- Calamity Ganon Speech

After Link activates the tower and it rises from the ground, the Old Man glides down on his Paraglider:

> "My, my... It would seem that we have quite the enigma here. This tower and several others just like it have erupted across the land, one after another. It is almost as though... a long-dormant power has awoken quite suddenly."

### About Calamity Ganon

> "I assume you caught sight of that atrocity enshrouding the castle."

> "That... is Calamity Ganon."

> "One hundred years ago, that vile entity brought the kingdom of Hyrule to ruin. It appeared suddenly and destroyed everything in its path. So many innocent lives were lost in its wake."

> "For a century, the very symbol of our kingdom, Hyrule Castle, has managed to contain that evil. But just barely. There it festers, building its strength for the moment it will unleash its blight upon the land once again."

### About Leaving the Plateau

> "Here, on this isolated plateau, we are surrounded on all sides by steep cliffs, with no way down. If you were to try to jump off, well... no death could be more certain. Or more foolish."

> "Of course, if you had a paraglider like mine, that would be quite another story."

### Nonverbal Cues
- Arrives by paraglider -- shows, doesn't tell
- "Quite the enigma" -- frames mystery, doesn't solve it
- Drops the paraglider hint casually, almost as afterthought
- Never says "you need my paraglider" -- just "that would be quite another story"

## 6. The Shrine Quest -- Paraglider Exchange

### The Deal

> "Do you see that structure there? The one shining with a strange light? It began glowing at the exact moment those towers rose up from the ground. I would think such a place might house some sort of treasure, wouldn't you?"

> "Treasure for the paraglider. A fair exchange, I believe."

### After Completing the First Shrine

> "It seems you managed to get your hands on a Spirit Orb. Well done!"

### Directing to More Shrines

> "These shrines are tucked away in numerous places all across this land. On this plateau alone, I believe there are still three more. Bring me the treasure from each of those shrines... and I will give you my paraglider."

### Nonverbal Cues
- "I would think... wouldn't you?" -- rhetorical question, not a command
- "A fair exchange, I believe" -- frames it as a deal between equals
- "Well done!" -- genuine praise, brief, no over-celebration

## 7. The Warm Doublet Quest -- Cold and Cooking

### Method A: The Recipe Quest (Old Man's Diary)

Found in the Old Man's cabin, his diary reads:

> "I do not know how I allowed this to happen, but it seems I forgot to write down a very important recipe. I know it contained raw meat and spicy pepper. However... I simply cannot remember what else I used!"

> "If I did find someone who knew the missing ingredient, I would happily reward them with my warm doublet. However, it seems unlikely that such a miraculous wish will ever be fulfilled."

After Link cooks the Spicy Meat and Seafood Fry and presents it:

*(The diary updates)*

> "Ah, and I will now write down this perfect recipe so that I will never forget it. Place some raw meat, spicy pepper, and hyrule bass in a pot, and then turn up the heat. Voila!"

*The Old Man rewards Link with the Warm Doublet.*

### Method B: Reaching Mount Hylia Summit Without It

If Link climbs to the peak of Mount Hylia without the Warm Doublet, the Old Man is waiting there and is impressed that Link made it without proper attire. He gives the Warm Doublet as a reward for the feat.

### Guidance About Cold

The Old Man hints about Spicy Peppers growing near his cabin and their warming properties, nudging Link toward the cooking system without spelling it out.

### Nonverbal Cues
- The diary is a *hint system disguised as a personal journal*
- He doesn't tell you the recipe -- he tells you he *forgot* part of it
- Multiple paths to the same reward (recipe OR summit climb)
- He rewards *initiative*, not obedience

## 8. After All Four Shrines -- The Summons

> "With this, you have now acquired all of the Spirit Orbs from the shrines on this plateau."

> "Oho ho! Extraordinary!"

> "That means... it is finally time. Link, it is finally time for me to tell you everything."

> "Imagine an X on your map, with the four shrines as the end points. Find the spot where those lines intersect. I shall wait for you there."

### Nonverbal Cues
- "Oho ho! Extraordinary!" -- genuine delight, the mask slipping
- Uses Link's name for the first time -- weight of the moment
- Even now, he doesn't just *tell* you where to go -- he gives a *puzzle*
- "I shall wait for you there" -- still pulling, not pushing

## 9. The Reveal -- Temple of Time Rooftop

### The Transformation

> "Well done there, young one! Now, then... The time has come to show you who I truly am."

> "I was King Rhoam Bosphoramus Hyrule. I was... the last leader of Hyrule. A kingdom which no longer exists."

### The Explanation

> "The Great Calamity was merciless. It devastated everything in its path, lo, a century ago. It was then that my life was taken away from me. And since that time, here I have remained, in spirit form."

> "I did not think it wise to overwhelm you while your memory was still fragile. So rather than that, I thought it best to assume a temporary form. Forgive me."

> "I think you are now ready. Ready to hear what happened 100 years ago."

### The History

> "To know Calamity Ganon's true form, one must know the story from an age long past..."

> "He appeared from deep below Hyrule Castle, seized control of the Guardians and the Divine Beasts, and turned them against us."

> "The Champions lost their lives. Those residing in the castle as well."

> "The appointed knight, gravely wounded, collapsed while defending the princess."

> "And thus, the kingdom of Hyrule was devastated absolutely by Calamity Ganon."

### The Personal Revelation

> "However... The princess survived... to face Ganon alone..."

> "That princess was my own daughter... My dear Zelda."

> "And the courageous knight who protected her right up to the very end... That knight was none other than you, Link."

> "You fought valiantly when your fate took an unfortunate turn. And then, you were taken to the Shrine of Resurrection. Here you now stand revitalized, 100 years later."

### Zelda's Voice

> "The words of guidance you have been hearing since your awakening are from Princess Zelda herself. Even now, as she works to restrain Ganon from within Hyrule Castle, she calls out for your help."

> "However, my daughter's power will soon be exhausted. Once that happens, Ganon will freely regenerate himself and nothing will stop him from consuming our land."

### The Request

> "Considering that I could not save my own kingdom, I have no right to ask this of you, Link..."

> "But I am powerless here..."

> "You must save her... my daughter. And do whatever it takes to annihilate Ganon."

### The Guidance

> "Somehow, Ganon has maintained control over all four Divine Beasts, as well as those Guardians swarming around Hyrule Castle. I believe it would be quite reckless for you to head directly to the castle at this point."

> "I suggest... that you make your way east, out to one of the villages in the wilderness."

> "Follow the road out to Kakariko Village. There you will find the elder, Impa. She will tell you more about the path that lies ahead."

### Nonverbal Cues
- Transforms from hunched old man to regal king in golden light
- "I was... the last leader" -- the pause carries grief
- "Forgive me" -- genuine, not performative
- "I have no right to ask this" -- acknowledges his failure before making the ask
- "I suggest..." -- even as king, even in his final moments, he *suggests*
- He never commands Link. His last words are guidance, not orders.

## 10. King Rhoam's Journal -- Hyrule Castle Library

*Found in the Library inside Hyrule Castle, hidden behind a bookshelf (accessed via Magnesis Rune).*

### Entry 1: Zelda's Birth

> "Today, as the sun rose and a new day was born, my daughter, too, joined this sweet world. In keeping with the traditions of the royal family, I have decided to name her... Zelda."

> "I am not a man accustomed to frivolous musings, but now seems as good a time as any to begin my royal memorandum."

### Entry 2: The Queen's Death

> "My queen has left this world. Her death was so sudden and unexpected, I awaken most nights unsure if she is really gone."

### Entry 3: Zelda's Strength

> "Zelda never cried, never faltered. Not even during the royal funeral or later when she and I were alone with our grief."

> "I must assume her strength is a result of us repeatedly informing her of her duty to be a valiant and steady princess."

> "For a child of merely six years of age, her conduct was truly that of a born leader. Her strength gives me hope."

### Entry 4: The Father vs. The King

> "My heart breaks for Zelda, but I must act as a king, not a father."

> "I must order her to train relentlessly at the fountain."

### Entry 5: Final Reflections

> "If she comes back without success, then I shall speak kindly with her. Scolding is pointless now."

> "I forced 10 years of training on her... and after all that, it seems her power will stubbornly awaken some other way."

> "Perhaps I should encourage her to keep researching her beloved relics. They may just lead her to answers I can't provide."

> "For now, I sit anxiously, more a father than a king in this moment. I sit and await my daughter's return."

### Design Notes
- The journal reveals the *cost* of his composure
- "More a father than a king" -- the mask drops in writing
- He realizes too late that his method was wrong
- His regret is the emotional engine of the whole game

---

## 11. The Old Man's Diary -- Woodcutter's Cabin

*Separate from King Rhoam's Journal. Found in the cabin on the Great Plateau.*

The diary contains his recipe notes (see Section 7) and hints at a simple life of solitude -- woodcutting, cooking, watching the plateau. It's the persona's journal, not the king's.

## 12. Guidance Style Analysis -- Merlin Design Patterns

### Pattern: Question, Don't Tell
- "I would think such a place might house some sort of treasure, wouldn't you?"
- "Answering a question with a question. That is fair enough."

### Pattern: Show, Don't Explain
- Arrives on paraglider (shows it works before offering it)
- Chops trees (demonstrates before teaching)
- Sits at campfire (models rest before suggesting it)

### Pattern: Reward Initiative, Not Obedience
- Mount Hylia summit = Warm Doublet for bravery
- Recipe quest = Warm Doublet for curiosity
- Both paths are equally valid

### Pattern: Humor as Trust-Building
- "I BEG YOUR PARDON!" then "Oho ho! Forgive me"
- "Toasty." (when set on fire)
- Playful mischief signals safety

### Pattern: Timed Reveals
- Never dumps information all at once
- Each shrine earns more context
- Name reveal comes last, after trust is built
- Even the final speech is layered: history, then personal, then request

### Pattern: The Suggest, Not Command
- "I suggest... that you make your way east"
- "You may take my advice or leave it"
- "Feel free to relax here for as long as you'd like"
- "A fair exchange, I believe"

### Pattern: Present, Not Pushing
- He's just *there* at the campfire, at the cabin, at the summit
- Link comes to him; he doesn't chase Link
- "I shall wait for you there" -- the quintessential Rhoam line
